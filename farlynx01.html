About

George G. Hamilton received his degree in Design in 1983, and has been working with many professional kitchens from then on. His work in designing our modular system of kitchen units has made a major difference in the availability of solutions in both the professional and home markets. Many of his works have been used in professional publications, and the designs are incorporated into the kitchen designs in motor homes.

Our kitchen modules are based on standard cabinet height, so we can use the basic construction plans to insure that the things fit.

Tools are made from silicon (do we need to go into this?) and are not going to melt, even when using some of them to turn food on a grill. The possibility of adding designs for the larger customers need to be mentioned, as well as the color ranges.

Baking forms that we do are the high-end, one of a kind forms that will usually not be reused. For this reason, we use a thin-walled, pre-treated form that will minimize sticking of both frozen and hot desserts. With the possibility of using silicon for smaller ones.

Mike Garand started his business making the pan forms back in high school, and has gone on to create many custom forms, including a large multi-piece one used to replicate Bart Simpson for the anniversary dinner. His work in the past has been used as a basis for ice sculpting, the The San Francisco Ice Cream Festival, and custom small pies for the 150th birthday dinner at the Abe Lincoln Presidential Library Foundation. His first pan was one invented to assist in making a chess cake.
